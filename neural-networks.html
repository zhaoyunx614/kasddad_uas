Neural networks

Multilayer perceptrons, activation functions, and the backpropagation algorithm — the deepest topic of KASDD, broken into bite-sized pieces.

1. Motivation — feature learning

Linear models fail on data that isn't linearly separable (e.g., concentric circles). Solution: transform $\phi(\mathbf{x})$ into a new space where the data becomes linearly separable:

$$\mathbf{y} = g(\mathbf{W}\phi(\mathbf{x})).$$

Designing $\phi$ by hand is hard. NN idea: $\phi$ is learned together with the final classifier.

2. MLP architecture

An $L$-layer feedforward NN. With $\mathbf{a}^{(0)} = \mathbf{x}$ and for $\ell = 1, \ldots, L$:

Forward pass $$\mathbf{p}^{(\ell)} = \mathbf{W}^{(\ell)}\mathbf{a}^{(\ell - 1)} + \mathbf{b}^{(\ell)} \quad \text{(pre-activation)},$$ $$\mathbf{a}^{(\ell)} = g^{(\ell)}(\mathbf{p}^{(\ell)}) \quad \text{(activation)}.$$
Where: $L$ = number of layers; $\mathbf{a}^{(\ell)}$ = output of layer $\ell$ (also input to layer $\ell+1$); $\mathbf{p}^{(\ell)}$ = pre-activation (linear part before nonlinearity); $\mathbf{W}^{(\ell)}, \mathbf{b}^{(\ell)}$ = weights & biases; $g^{(\ell)}$ = activation function.

If $\dim(\mathbf{a}^{(\ell)}) = D_\ell$, then $\mathbf{W}^{(\ell)} \in \mathbb{R}^{D_\ell \times D_{\ell-1}}$ and $\mathbf{b}^{(\ell)} \in \mathbb{R}^{D_\ell}$.

3. Counting parameters

Total parameter count $$\sum_{\ell=1}^{L} (D_\ell D_{\ell-1} + D_\ell).$$
Typical exam problem — counting dimensions

Problem. A FFNN for 3-class classification. Input has 13 features, first layer has 23 units.

Layer 1 (input → 23 units): $\mathbf{W}^{(1)} \in \mathbb{R}^{23 \times 13}$, $\mathbf{b}^{(1)} \in \mathbb{R}^{23}$. Total: $23 \cdot 13 + 23 = 322$.

Layer 2 (23 → 3 output): $\mathbf{W}^{(2)} \in \mathbb{R}^{3 \times 23}$, $\mathbf{b}^{(2)} \in \mathbb{R}^{3}$. Total: $3 \cdot 23 + 3 = 72$.

Final-layer activation: Softmax (3 mutually exclusive classes).

Grand total: $322 + 72 = 394$ parameters.

General rule: rows of $\mathbf{W}^{(\ell)}$ = units in layer $\ell$; columns = units in previous layer. Bias matches units in its own layer.

4. Activation functions — complete catalog

For each: the function $g(u)$, its derivative $g'(u)$, range, and typical use.

1 · Identity / Linear $$g(u) = u, \qquad g'(u) = 1.$$

Range: $(-\infty, \infty)$. Use: regression output.

2 · Sigmoid $$g(u) = \text{Sig}(u) = \frac{1}{1 + e^{-u}}, \qquad g'(u) = g(u)(1 - g(u)).$$

Range: $(0, 1)$. Use: binary classification output. Risk: vanishing gradients for large $|u|$.

3 · Tanh $$g(u) = \tanh(u) = \frac{e^u - e^{-u}}{e^u + e^{-u}}, \qquad g'(u) = 1 - \tanh^2(u) = 1 - g(u)^2.$$

Range: $(-1, 1)$. Use: zero-centered hidden activations.

4 · ReLU $$g(u) = \max(0, u), \qquad g'(u) = \begin{cases} 1 & u > 0 \\ 0 & u < 0 \end{cases}.$$

Range: $[0, \infty)$. Use: default hidden layer in deep nets. Risk: dying ReLU (neurons stuck at 0).

5 · Leaky ReLU $$g(u) = \begin{cases} u & u \geq 0 \\ \alpha u & u < 0 \end{cases}, \qquad g'(u) = \begin{cases} 1 & u \geq 0 \\ \alpha & u < 0 \end{cases}.$$

$\alpha$ small (typically 0.01). Use: avoid dying ReLU.

6 · Softplus (smooth ReLU) $$g(u) = \ln(1 + e^u), \qquad g'(u) = \frac{1}{1 + e^{-u}} = \text{Sig}(u).$$

Range: $(0, \infty)$. Key fact: its derivative is the sigmoid. Appears often in exam problems!

7 · ELU $$g(u) = \begin{cases} u & u > 0 \\ \alpha(e^u - 1) & u \leq 0 \end{cases}, \qquad g'(u) = \begin{cases} 1 & u > 0 \\ \alpha e^u & u \leq 0 \end{cases}.$$

Range: $(-\alpha, \infty)$. Pushes mean activations toward 0.

8 · Softmax (vector → simplex) $$g(\mathbf{u})_k = \text{softmax}_k(\mathbf{u}) = \frac{e^{u_k}}{\sum_{\ell=1}^{K} e^{u_\ell}}.$$

Range: simplex (each component in $(0,1)$, sum = 1). Use: multi-class output.

Jacobian: $\dfrac{\partial s_k}{\partial u_j} = s_k(\mathbb{1}[k=j] - s_j)$.

Hidden-layer activation — which one?
  • Default modern choice: ReLU (fast, simple, works in most cases).
  • Suspect dying ReLU? Leaky ReLU or ELU.
  • Want zero-centered hidden representations? Tanh.
  • Want smooth differentiability? Softplus or sigmoid.

5. Loss functions for neural networks

The four loss functions you need

Univariate regression: $L = \tfrac{1}{2}(a^{(L)} - y)^2$

Multivariate regression: $L = \tfrac{1}{2}(\mathbf{a}^{(L)} - \mathbf{y})^\top(\mathbf{a}^{(L)} - \mathbf{y})$

Binary classification (BCE): $L = -y \log a^{(L)} - (1 - y)\log(1 - a^{(L)})$

Multi-class (CCE): $L = -\sum_{k=1}^{K} y_k \log a^{(L)}_k = -\mathbf{y}^\top \log \mathbf{a}^{(L)}$

Multi-label: $L = -\sum_k \left[y_k \log a^{(L)}_k + (1 - y_k)\log(1 - a^{(L)}_k)\right]$

6. The universal output-layer shortcut

When activation + loss are matched, magic happens

For all three standard pairings — and only these — the gradient with respect to the pre-activation collapses to prediction − target:

$$\boxed{\;\overline{\mathbf{p}}^{(L)} = \mathbf{a}^{(L)} - \mathbf{y}\;}$$

This holds when:

  • Identity + MSE
  • Sigmoid + BCE
  • Softmax + CCE

This is why these three pairings are the standard — the math is the cleanest.

7. Backpropagation

Backprop is the chain rule applied carefully. Let $\overline{v} = \dfrac{\partial L}{\partial v}$ (the adjoint / gradient w.r.t. $v$). For a 2-layer network:

Forward (2-layer)

$$\mathbf{u}^{(1)} = \mathbf{W}^{(1)}\mathbf{x}, \quad \mathbf{p}^{(1)} = \mathbf{u}^{(1)} + \mathbf{b}^{(1)}, \quad \mathbf{a}^{(1)} = g^{(1)}(\mathbf{p}^{(1)}),$$ $$\mathbf{u}^{(2)} = \mathbf{W}^{(2)}\mathbf{a}^{(1)}, \quad \mathbf{p}^{(2)} = \mathbf{u}^{(2)} + \mathbf{b}^{(2)}, \quad \mathbf{a}^{(2)} = g^{(2)}(\mathbf{p}^{(2)}), \quad L = L(\mathbf{a}^{(2)}, \mathbf{y}).$$

Backward

Backward pass (2-layer) $$\overline{L} = 1,$$ $$\overline{\mathbf{a}}^{(2)} = \overline{L} \cdot \frac{\partial L}{\partial \mathbf{a}^{(2)}}, \quad \overline{\mathbf{p}}^{(2)} = \overline{\mathbf{a}}^{(2)} \odot g^{(2)\prime}(\mathbf{p}^{(2)}),$$ $$\overline{\mathbf{b}}^{(2)} = \overline{\mathbf{p}}^{(2)}, \quad \overline{\mathbf{W}}^{(2)} = \overline{\mathbf{p}}^{(2)}(\mathbf{a}^{(1)})^\top,$$ $$\overline{\mathbf{a}}^{(1)} = (\mathbf{W}^{(2)})^\top \overline{\mathbf{p}}^{(2)}, \quad \overline{\mathbf{p}}^{(1)} = \overline{\mathbf{a}}^{(1)} \odot g^{(1)\prime}(\mathbf{p}^{(1)}),$$ $$\overline{\mathbf{b}}^{(1)} = \overline{\mathbf{p}}^{(1)}, \quad \overline{\mathbf{W}}^{(1)} = \overline{\mathbf{p}}^{(1)} \mathbf{x}^\top.$$
Where: $\odot$ = elementwise (Hadamard) product; $g^{(\ell)\prime}$ = derivative of activation at layer $\ell$.

Universal recipe (L-layer)

Memorize this — same pattern at every layer

For $\ell = L, L-1, \ldots, 1$:

$$\overline{\mathbf{W}}^{(\ell)} = \overline{\mathbf{p}}^{(\ell)}(\mathbf{a}^{(\ell-1)})^\top,$$ $$\overline{\mathbf{b}}^{(\ell)} = \overline{\mathbf{p}}^{(\ell)},$$ $$\overline{\mathbf{a}}^{(\ell-1)} = (\mathbf{W}^{(\ell)})^\top \overline{\mathbf{p}}^{(\ell)},$$ $$\overline{\mathbf{p}}^{(\ell-1)} = \overline{\mathbf{a}}^{(\ell-1)} \odot g^{(\ell-1)\prime}(\mathbf{p}^{(\ell-1)}).$$

Then update: $\mathbf{W}^{(\ell)} := \mathbf{W}^{(\ell)} - \alpha\,\overline{\mathbf{W}}^{(\ell)}$, $\mathbf{b}^{(\ell)} := \mathbf{b}^{(\ell)} - \alpha\,\overline{\mathbf{b}}^{(\ell)}$.

Derivative substitutions by activation

Activation$g^{(\ell)\prime}(\mathbf{p}^{(\ell)})$ to plug in
Identity$\mathbf{1}$ (vector of ones)
Sigmoid$\mathbf{a}^{(\ell)} \odot (1 - \mathbf{a}^{(\ell)})$
Tanh$1 - \mathbf{a}^{(\ell)} \odot \mathbf{a}^{(\ell)}$
ReLU$\mathbb{1}[\mathbf{p}^{(\ell)} > 0]$ (0/1 mask)
Leaky ReLU$\mathbb{1}[\mathbf{p}^{(\ell)} > 0] + \alpha\,\mathbb{1}[\mathbf{p}^{(\ell)} < 0]$
Softplus$\text{Sig}(\mathbf{p}^{(\ell)})$
ELU$\mathbb{1}[\mathbf{p}^{(\ell)} > 0] + \alpha e^{\mathbf{p}^{(\ell)}} \mathbb{1}[\mathbf{p}^{(\ell)} \leq 0]$
Mental model

The backward pass moves right-to-left, and at each step you unwind the forward operation. Each forward step "multiply by W → add b → apply g" becomes in reverse:

  1. Multiply gradient elementwise by $g'$,
  2. Copy to $\overline{\mathbf{b}}$,
  3. Outer-product with input for $\overline{\mathbf{W}}$,
  4. Multiply by $\mathbf{W}^\top$ to pass to previous layer.

8. Worked example — numerical backprop (tanh + identity, MSE)

2-layer FFNN. Input $\mathbf{x} = (-1, 1)^\top$, target $y = 1$. Layer 1: 3 tanh units. Layer 2: identity (univariate regression).

$$\mathbf{W}^{(1)} = \begin{pmatrix} 0.5 & -0.5 \\ 1 & 0.3 \\ -0.3 & 0.6 \end{pmatrix}, \quad \mathbf{b}^{(1)} = \begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix},$$ $$\mathbf{W}^{(2)} = (0.5,\, 0.2,\, 0.3), \quad b^{(2)} = 0.3.$$

Forward

$$\mathbf{u}^{(1)} = (-1, -0.7, 0.9)^\top, \quad \mathbf{p}^{(1)} = (-2, 0.3, 1.9)^\top,$$ $$\mathbf{a}^{(1)} = (\tanh(-2), \tanh(0.3), \tanh(1.9))^\top = (-0.964, 0.291, 0.956)^\top,$$ $$u^{(2)} = -0.482 + 0.0582 + 0.2868 = -0.137, \quad p^{(2)} = -0.137 + 0.3 = 0.163,$$ $$a^{(2)} = 0.163, \quad L = \tfrac{1}{2}(0.163 - 1)^2 = 0.35.$$

Backward

$$\overline{a}^{(2)} = 0.163 - 1 = -0.837, \quad \overline{p}^{(2)} = -0.837,$$ $$\overline{\mathbf{W}}^{(2)} = -0.837 \cdot (-0.964, 0.291, 0.956) = (0.807, -0.244, -0.800),$$ $$\overline{\mathbf{a}}^{(1)} = -0.837 \cdot (0.5, 0.2, 0.3) = (-0.418, -0.167, -0.251),$$ $$g^{(1)\prime} = 1 - \mathbf{a}^{(1)\odot 2} = (0.071, 0.915, 0.086),$$ $$\overline{\mathbf{p}}^{(1)} = (-0.030, -0.153, -0.021),$$ $$\overline{\mathbf{W}}^{(1)} = \overline{\mathbf{p}}^{(1)} \mathbf{x}^\top = \begin{pmatrix} 0.030 & -0.030 \\ 0.153 & -0.153 \\ 0.021 & -0.021 \end{pmatrix}.$$

9. Worked example — softplus + softmax (CCE)

2-layer FFNN, layer 1 softplus (2 hidden units), layer 2 softmax (3 classes). Input $\mathbf{x} = (1, -1, 1)$, target class 1 (one-hot $\mathbf{y} = (0, 1, 0)$).

$$\mathbf{W}^{(1)} = \begin{pmatrix} 0.5 & -0.5 & 0.8 \\ 1 & -0.2 & -0.6 \end{pmatrix}, \quad \mathbf{b}^{(1)} = \begin{pmatrix} 0.1 \\ -0.1 \end{pmatrix},$$ $$\mathbf{W}^{(2)} = \begin{pmatrix} 0.2 & -0.1 \\ 0.4 & 0.2 \\ 0.3 & -0.2 \end{pmatrix}, \quad \mathbf{b}^{(2)} = \begin{pmatrix} 0.1 \\ 0.1 \\ -0.1 \end{pmatrix}.$$

Forward layer 1

$$\mathbf{p}^{(1)} = \mathbf{W}^{(1)}\mathbf{x} + \mathbf{b}^{(1)} = (1.9, 0.5),$$ $$\mathbf{a}^{(1)} = (\ln(1 + e^{1.9}), \ln(1 + e^{0.5})) = (2.040, 0.974).$$

Forward layer 2 + softmax

$$\mathbf{p}^{(2)} = \mathbf{W}^{(2)}\mathbf{a}^{(1)} + \mathbf{b}^{(2)} \approx (0.310, 1.151, 0.317),$$ $$\mathbf{a}^{(2)} = \text{softmax}(\mathbf{p}^{(2)}) \approx (0.24, 0.55, 0.24).$$

Backward (softmax + CCE shortcut)

$$\overline{\mathbf{p}}^{(2)} = \mathbf{a}^{(2)} - \mathbf{y} \approx (0.24, -0.45, 0.24).$$

Layer 1 gradient (using softplus derivative = sigmoid):

$$g^{(1)\prime}(\mathbf{p}^{(1)}) = (\text{Sig}(1.9), \text{Sig}(0.5)) = (0.870, 0.622),$$ $$\overline{\mathbf{a}}^{(1)} = (\mathbf{W}^{(2)})^\top \overline{\mathbf{p}}^{(2)}, \quad \overline{\mathbf{p}}^{(1)} = \overline{\mathbf{a}}^{(1)} \odot g^{(1)\prime}(\mathbf{p}^{(1)}).$$

After computation: $\overline{\mathbf{p}}^{(1)} \approx (-0.065, -0.105)$.

🏀 NBA — MVP voting share with MLP

2-layer FFNN with sigmoid output. Input (5 features): PPG, RPG, APG, FG%, Win%. Layer 1: 8 hidden units (ReLU). Layer 2: 1 sigmoid unit.

Parameter shapes: $\mathbf{W}^{(1)} \in \mathbb{R}^{8\times 5}, \mathbf{b}^{(1)} \in \mathbb{R}^8, \mathbf{W}^{(2)} \in \mathbb{R}^{1\times 8}, b^{(2)} \in \mathbb{R}$. Total: $40 + 8 + 8 + 1 = 57$ parameters.

For Nikola Jokić's 2023–24 season: $\mathbf{x} = (26.4, 12.4, 9.0, 58.3, 68.3)$. Suppose $p^{(2)} = 2.45$ after the forward pass:

$$a^{(2)} = \text{Sig}(2.45) = \frac{1}{1 + e^{-2.45}} \approx 0.92.$$

92% predicted MVP share — matches the actual MVP voting that season.
